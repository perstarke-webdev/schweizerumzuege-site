## Design Context

### Users
Schweizer Umzuege serves private households and businesses in Switzerland, with a practical focus on Bern, Basel, and Zurich. The core audience is typically 30+, price-aware, and decision-oriented. They arrive during stressful moments where they need a provider that looks credible immediately, explains the service clearly, and makes it easy to request an offer or call directly.

Typical jobs to be done:
- get a trustworthy quote quickly
- understand which services are offered
- decide whether the company feels reliable enough to contact
- find direct contact details without friction

The interface should reduce stress, create confidence fast, and make the next step obvious.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel premium and approachable.

Core brand traits:
- Swiss precise
- calm and structured
- trustworthy and human

The tone should communicate competence without feeling cold, luxurious without becoming exclusive, and helpful without looking cheap or overly sales-driven.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light visual system rooted in the existing brand identity: red, white, and charcoal from the current logo and live site. The visual language should evolve the current repo direction, not the old production site's UI patterns.

Reference direction:
- preserve the recognizable brand palette and factual business content from the live site
- keep the current repo's cleaner, more editorial, trust-first direction

Anti-direction:
- do not repeat the old live site's dense layout, generic lead-gen feel, or form-heavy hero
- do not introduce playful, flashy, or decorative design moves that weaken credibility

The desired result is a refined Swiss service aesthetic: precise, premium, readable, and warm enough to feel approachable.

### Design Principles
1. Trust must be visible immediately.
Show proof, service clarity, phone access, and the primary offer CTA early. Visitors should understand who the company helps, where it operates, and how to get started within seconds.

2. Premium should come from precision, not luxury cliches.
Use strong typography, disciplined spacing, realistic imagery, and restrained motion. Avoid glossy effects, visual clutter, or trendy flourishes that feel generic.

3. Approachability matters as much as authority.
The site should feel competent and polished, but never cold. Clear copy, calm pacing, and human contact options should make the company feel easy to work with.

4. Content accuracy overrides invention.
When copy, service claims, proof points, or business facts are touched, validate them against the approved repo content and the live site baseline before changing them.

5. Performance and SEO are product requirements.
Design and implementation should support excellent crawlability, semantic structure, fast rendering, optimized media, minimal JS, and a Lighthouse target of 100 where practical across performance, accessibility, best practices, and SEO.
